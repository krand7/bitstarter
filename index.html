Since the early 2000's, nursing homes have seen a dramatic decrease in the number of adequate nursing staff members. Through a variety of factors--including added pressure through understaffing--the call for nurses to pursue careers caring for the elderly population has become less and less favorable. And as this trend continues, an obvious negative trend is occurring on the quality of care given to patients in these homes. While this is certainly not a problem that can be tackled overnight, our mission is to relieve, piece-by-piece, the massive stress placed on nurses and, through this, improve the care given to our family members and friends.

As a first step, we aim to integrate current commercial technologies with nursing home infrastructure to provide a simple system for automating patients' medications. One of the most difficult parts of the transition between receiving in-home care from a loved one and receiving full-time care from a nurse is the shift into a lack of full recognition. While patients may understand that nurses qualify to do their job, many, especially those suffering from dementia or Alzheimer's Disease, may be unable to place their trust in the new face of a nurse. Additionally, the role of nurse's to administer medication to each patient with a multitude of separate regimens is an intensive responsibility that should already be simplified. We aim to introduce technology through Microsoft's Kinect and the associated FaceShift software to create a system that automates medication regimens with a personal touch. For each admitted patient, a loved one will perform a short series of pseudo-interactive voice recordings that remind the patient to take their medicine, and simultaneously record and animate their facial patterns to create a personalized 3D avatar to be used within that patient's room. A central control panel will automate these recordings and the access to the specified pills at the necessary times in the day.

FAQ

How will the avatars match the physical appearance of the human giving the voice recordings?
The FaceShift program does a fantastic job of matching facial structures in the avatar, but we will create a separate database of different skin textures and hairstyles to best match the full face.
There are way more details to a face than bone structure, skin color, and hair--how will you accompany for other things?
Research shows that the region of the brain responsible for facial recognition has a strong ability to recognize generalized facial structures. If you have suggestions for specific details that we should keep in mind, we would love it if you sent us an email!
Will nursing homes be able to maintain this system as more and more patients are emitted?
Once the system has reached an acceptable level of functionality, we plan to split our time between continuing system evolution and developing an attractive and easy-to-understand user-interface.
